Rider Support Roles

We need to allocate roles as we can only have 3 drivers on each camper to ensure that we always have a HQ Driver and a Sleeper Driver in each group

Support Role After Riding Before Riding Duties
HQ Driver Off duty in sleeper Driving HQ and supporting riders Since the HQ will also provide support for riders this is a busy role
Sleeper Driver Drive Sleeper then off duty in sleeper Relax in HQ Driving Sleeper when off duty, approximately 1/3 of off duty shift.
Captain Off duty in sleeper Organise the team in the camperHQ to ensure slick operation Delegating to any other available which may include the Captain role once settled into routine. Tasks are listed below
Support Off duty in sleeper Supporting HQ driver or logistics if needed Helping out with anything needed

Tasks to be done / delegated by Captain

  • Navigation. Ensure route to the next changeover is clear to Riders, Sleeper, HQ Driver, HQ Driver Support
  • Organising timely food for riders coming off shift
  • Ensuring riders coming on shift are ready for changeover
  • Ensuring bikes are unloaded and ready for riders coming on shift
  • Ensuring bikes coming off shift are loaded onto free rack
  • Ensuring all lights are put on charge after coming off shift
  • Anything else for smooth running

The detailed proposed schedule for each role is detailed in rota page here ... Rota

Groupings / Roles

  • We need to have rough groupings to ensure that we allocate roles approximately evenly, it would be no good having all CamperHQ drivers in one group for example
  • After a couple of shifts when everybody is getting settled we can move riders up or down as requested by those wanting to move up a group for a few rotations
  • We are top and middle heavy ( no pun intended for those who are more gravitationally challenged ), more riders fit the A B group speeds. But everybody is happy to mix it up with other groups which makes a more sociable trip. And its easier to move riders down than move riders up.
  • Some riders are stronger on the flat and less strong on the hills so we may mix up the groups according to the up coming terrain
  • If we have less than 4 riders in any group we will take turns to fill the group from other groups.
