Groupings / Roles

  • We need to have rough groupings to ensure that we allocate roles approximately evenly, it would be no good having all HQ drivers in one group for example
  • After a couple of shifts when everybody is getting settled we can move riders up or down as requested by those wanting to move up a group for a few rotations
  • We are top and middle heavy ( no pun intended for those who are more gravitationally challenged ), more riders fit the A B group speeds. But everybody is happy to mix it up with other groups which makes a more sociable trip. And its easier to move riders down than move riders up.
  • Some riders are stronger on the flat and less strong on the hills so we may mix up the groups according to the up coming terrain
  • If we have less than 4 riders in any group we will take turns to fill the group from other groups.

Swapping Riders

We need to be able to swap between groups, especially filling the C group. Or allowing riders to move up a group for extra challenge. And we do want to mix the groups up though for maximum social and this takes some careful thought, to ensure that we always have a driver for CamperHQ and Sleeper

  • Initially one rider from Dan, Seens and Tony will drop down to ride with the C group. All of which are HQ-Drivers so fills the gap nicely with each group made up with one person for each role
  • Once settled into a rhythm, the Logistics role and Van driver roles should be easy to swap round. So some possible easy swaps outlined below.
  • CG (Logistics) could ride with C group and Matt (Logistics) or Togle (Van) could move up to fill my spot and the spare HQ-Driver ( Dan or Seens ) from B-group filling the gap left by Matt or Togle.
  • Stu (Van) could ride with C group and Togle (Van) could move up to fill Stus spot and Dan or Seens (Both HQ-Driver) from B-group filling the gap left by Togle.
  • Matt (Logistics) could ride with C group and Dan or Seens (Both HQ-Driver) fill Matts spot
  • Tim (Van) could ride with C group and Dan or Seens (Both HQ-Driver) fill Togles spot.
  • So I think the only tricky things are for the sleeper camper drivers Mike and Tim Narey to ride with the C group. Or maybe TimN moves up to A, Merv moves up to B and Mike moves down to C group. So options.
